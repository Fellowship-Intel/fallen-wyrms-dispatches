# Canon & Voice Brief (for writing Dispatches content)

## The world in three sentences
The world was made a paradise, tended by gods who dwelt in it. One of them turned to the Void, split the divine host, and broke the world; the loyal gods withdrew by choice, and the magic they left behind is fading. This is the Age of Receding Gods: the light is running out, and something is moving in the thin places.

## The frame: the Chronicler
Every dispatch is narrated or framed by the Chronicler, in truth Silvara, the withdrawn goddess of truth and memory. She takes no side and lifts no hand; she only witnesses and records, so that when the light is gone there is still a true account of how it went out. Her owls are her eyes across the world.

Voice: calm, precise, weighty, elegiac. Plain sentences that carry their own gravity. One quiet flash of feeling, then move on. Never hype, tease, exclaim, or use emojis. Never use em dashes.

## The three viewpoints in Season One
- **The keeper of the Everflame** (the Hallowed South): has tended the last great mainland flame for sixty years; watches it fail on the opening night.
- **Corvane, the Ranger of the North** (the Failing Marches): a weathered guardian who finds proof the dark is not leaking but being guided, and is not believed.
- **Wick, a hearth-brownie** (the Deepwood's edge): a small fading spirit, made of the world's ambient magic, the first to feel the light thin.

## Player-safe rule
Keep major novel reveals off the page (secret identities, the enemy's true endgame). The known-in-world truth about the watch-lights ("they were never a promise, only lights") is used sparingly as the season's closing turn.

## Fuller canon
The complete world lives in the project's World Bible and the illustrated compendia. Ask the owner for the GM edition if you need hidden canon; do not invent cosmology that contradicts the above.
